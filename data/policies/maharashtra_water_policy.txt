Maharashtra Groundwater ConservationThe Maharashtra Groundwater (Development and Management) Act of 2009 was established to ensure a sustainable and equitable supply of groundwater. It moves beyond just protecting drinking water to regulating the total use of the resource across irrigation and industry. Key rules include the prohibition of drilling deep wells (over 60 meters) in notified areas and the mandatory registration of all existing wells. In water-scarce or over-exploited watersheds, the state can regulate the types of crops grown and limit water extraction to prevent the depletion of critical aquifers.PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana) GuidelinesThe overarching vision of PMKSY is to ensure "Har Khet Ko Pani" (Water for Every Farm) and "Per Drop More Crop." The guidelines focus on a decentralized planning process where states create District Irrigation Plans (DIPs) to identify gaps in water infrastructure. A major component is the promotion of micro-irrigation technologies like drip and sprinkler systems, providing subsidies up to 55% for small and marginal farmers. The program emphasizes the convergence of various water-related schemes to improve on-farm water use efficiency and reduce dependency on seasonal monsoons.Crop Recommendations for Drought-Prone AreasFor regions in Maharashtra facing recurring water scarcity, experts recommend shifting away from water-intensive crops like sugarcane and toward drought-resistant varieties. Top recommendations include Pearl Millet (Bajra), Sorghum (Jowar), and Pigeon Pea (Tur), all of which have deep root systems or short growth cycles that allow them to thrive on minimal moisture. Additionally, specific stress-tolerant varieties such as the MAUS-158 soybean (short duration) and BDN-711 pigeon pea are specifically upscaled in regions like Marathwada to escape the peak of dry spells and ensure stable yields.
